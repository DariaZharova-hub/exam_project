About Piņķi

Where it is?

Piņķi, formerly Saliena, is a settlement in Babite municipality of Marupe district, the centre of the municipality. It is situated in the Baltic Sea region, at the junction of the A5 and A10 motorways, on the right bank of the Neriņa River, 12 km from the centre of the municipality of Marupe and 15 km from the centre of Riga. Includes the detached villages of Lielpriedes and Silarāji. Piņķi is the fourth largest village (2024) in Latvia in terms of population.

History

Historical facts about this city!

The area around Piņķi was first mentioned in written sources in 1225, and by a treaty of 16 March 1226 it was included in the Riga Rural District. In the 14th and 15th centuries, the manors of Pele, Beberbeki and Olekte were mentioned. The centre of the parish district of Piņķi (Kirchspiel Pinkenhof).

During the Baltic Russification in 1884, the highest level, the so-called Peter School, was established in the Nikolai School in Piņķi, where education was conducted in Russian. In 1883, there were also the Help Class School with 82 pupils, the Anna Kalnina School with 29 pupils, the Martin Amol Private School with 15 pupils and the German Parish School with 56 pupils.

During the Latvian freedom struggle, on 22 May 1919, units of the Latvian Separate Brigade fought with units of the Soviet Latvian Army near Piņķi. During the Bermontide, the 9th Rezekne Regiment of the Latgale Division fought against the 1st Plastun Regiment of the West Russian Army at Piņķi on 4 November 1919, as the Russians had landed across the Lielupe River. On 8 November, the 9th Rezekne Regiment recaptured the Bulduri Bridge and Jurmala up to the Dubulti Strait.

During the Latvian SSR, the Sovkhoz "Saliena" was established on the land of the Piņķi manor and in 1964 it was merged with the Bulduri Gardening Technical School to form the Bulduri Gardening Sovkhoztehnikum. The village of Saliena, with a secondary school and a cultural centre, was established in the centre of the Bulduri Sovkhoztehnikum. After the restoration of Latvia's independence, Piņķi regained its old name.
